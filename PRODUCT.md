## What this is

CASEMEX (Cafés y Semillas de México S.A. de C.V.) is a **B2B green (unroasted) coffee trader and exporter** from Chiapas, Mexico — 100% Mexican, 100% family-owned, third generation, operating since 1996. The site is a one-page vitrine, **not** a store and **not** a consumer brand.

**The 3-second test:** a visitor must understand "green coffee, Mexican, B2B, established" almost immediately. Killing the "is this a café / roaster / shop?" misread is priority #1.

## Users

Professional buyers in the coffee supply chain who value **reliability over price** and want long-term relationships, not one-off transactions:

- **Traders / commodities buyers** — need guaranteed volume, consistency, and on-time contract fulfillment.
- **Exporters** — need sourcing capacity, infrastructure, and traceability for international markets.
- **Roasters** (industrial → specialty) — need consistent quality, defined profiles, technical understanding.
- **Coffee brands** — need a serious, certified, traceable supplier with an origin story they can pass on.

Global audience; primary language Spanish, with English for international buyers. No fixed age range.

**Not for:** end consumers, anyone seeking roasted/ground coffee. CASEMEX does not roast.

## The job to be done

Convert trust into contact. Primary action: **request a quote** (form → eduardo.casemex@gmail.com) or **reach out on WhatsApp**. The buyer's core fear — _"will I get the coffee I need, in the quality, volume and time I need?"_ — must be answered everywhere: reliable supply, consistent quality, traceability, certification, the backing of 30 years.

## How it should feel

Solid, serious, backed by three decades — **and** warm, human, close. The opposite of cold, generic, investor-facing multinationals. Premium without being flashy; meticulous, not loud. The buyer should sense care and accountability, not a circus.

## Differentiator (the competitive weapon)

100% Mexican + 100% family + 3rd generation + 30 years (Chiapas, since 1996). Decisions are made locally, close to producer and client — agility, flexibility, genuinely personal treatment. Direct, long-term relationships with the growers who are "the soul of the business."

## Anti-references (do NOT replicate)

- **ECC** (ecc.com.mx) — logo soup, no context, errors; claims a foreign parent group's ~80-year history that isn't theirs. We state only our real, verifiable story (family-founded 1996, 30+ years).
- **Olam, LDC** — cold, generic, investor-focused commodity giants where a mid-size client is "a number." We are warm and human.
- General sector tells: outdated/neglected design, broken/empty/"under construction" sections, exposed internal tools, poor mobile experience, buried Mexican/family origin.

Admired references (clean, modern, ordered B2B green-coffee): **Caravela Coffee, Sucafina, Mercanta / The Coffee Hunters.**

## Hard restrictions

- **Green coffee only.** Never imply roasting or selling for consumption. No cups, espresso, latte, barista, café imagery — ever.
- **Only real, verifiable claims.** Founded 1996, 30+ years, third generation, family, "one of Mexico's largest coffee traders," 100+ collaborators, in-field agronomy team, consolidated network of hundreds of growers, the listed certifications. Do NOT invent volumes, awards, exact grower counts, market share, or borrow a foreign group's tenure. Unknowns are marked `{{TODO}}`, never faked.
- Don't bury the Mexican/family origin. Don't saturate with logos. No impersonal corporate tone. No "under construction."
- **Impeccable on mobile.** Performance is a top priority; the heavy hero video must be light/absent on phones.

## Design principles (guide every decision)

1. **Category clarity first.** "Green coffee, B2B, Mexican, since 1996" reads instantly — explicit kicker, no ambiguity.
2. **Warmth is the moat.** Every choice should feel like a family company that answers for its word, not a faceless corporate. Human, close, accountable.
3. **Quiet confidence.** Restraint over spectacle; meticulous detail signals the same meticulousness buyers want in their coffee. Motion has purpose and always honors `prefers-reduced-motion`.
4. **Earn trust with specifics.** Supply, consistency, traceability, certification — answer the buyer's fear with concrete guarantees, not adjectives. No buzzwords.
5. **One visual body.** The site is graded like the hero film — warm, earthy, documentary. Type, color, and imagery cohere into a single, premium whole.

## Accessibility

WCAG AA: contrast ≥4.5:1 body / ≥3:1 large & UI, semantic HTML and heading order, visible focus, labeled inputs, no color-only meaning, 44px touch targets, full keyboard access, reduced-motion respected. Bilingual ES/EN with correct `lang` and hreflang.

## Slogan & voice

Slogan: **"Todo es café."** (kept in Spanish across locales). Voice: warm first-person plural, sober and professional, short concrete sentences, concrete verbs over adjectives. Tone is formal but human.
